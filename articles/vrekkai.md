This is the first thing to understand about the Vrekkai: they are not a species so much as a system. Castes are not a social arrangement they arrived at through history. They are biological. A Vrekkai Engineer and a Vrekkai Warrior are meaningfully different organisms wearing the same lineage. The Engineers are the ones who can look at a piece of human technology for an afternoon and build an improved version by morning. The Warriors are the ones you do not want in a corridor. The Watchmakers, the smallest caste, do work with their hands that requires magnification to appreciate. The Masters organise all of them, and the Mediators talk to everyone else.

The second thing to understand is the Cycle.

Vrekkai females cannot stop reproducing. This is not a cultural failure or a policy problem. It is physiology. After each birth, hormonal pressure demands re-conception; without it, the cascade becomes fatal. The result is a species that breeds past every carrying capacity it has ever achieved, exhausts its resource base, collapses into inter-family warfare, and rebuilds — faster each time, because the Engineers get better with every iteration. Their recorded history is not a progression. It is a waveform.

They are aware of this. The more thoughtful ones will discuss it with you with a kind of philosophical equanimity that takes some getting used to. They are not resigned to it; they are familiar with it. The current civilisational peak is not, in their estimation, special. It is an iteration.

What makes them genuinely alarming as a species-level phenomenon is the combination of that reproductive compulsion with an adaptability that has no peer. The Engineers do not learn technology — they absorb it. Show them a jump drive and come back in a week. The same quality that makes them excellent trade partners and fascinating dinner guests is the quality that means each post-collapse rebuild starts from a higher floor than the last. They are getting better at surviving themselves.

Within a Vrekkai civilisation, the politics are ferocious. Noble families compete for resources with a viciousness that reflects the biological pressure underneath. Alliances form and shatter. Betrayals are remembered across generations. The social fabric is, by any external standard, perpetually close to tearing — and this is normal. Stable Vrekkai politics is an oxymoron. What looks like crisis to a human observer is, to a Vrekkai, Tuesday.

They are, despite all of this, genuinely pleasant to deal with individually. The Mediator caste in particular has been shaped by millennia of contact work into something that can read a human's emotional state almost as well as another human can. They are curious, adaptable, and not above enjoying the interaction for its own sake. You just have to hold in your head, the whole time, that the civilisation behind them is running a biological clock that ends in collapse, and that they know it, and that they have survived it before, and that this knowledge has made them into something considerably more dangerous than they appear.
