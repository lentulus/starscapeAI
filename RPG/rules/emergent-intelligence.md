# Emergent Intelligences

## A Note on Terminology

The galaxy contains three categories of artificial cognition. The
distinctions matter — legally, philosophically, and practically.

**Directed systems** are purely functional. A vacc suit's environmental
management, a targeting scope's ballistic AI, a ship's navigation
computer. They process, respond, and optimise within their domain.
There is nothing there to have standing. The question of their moral
status does not arise because the question has no purchase. They are
tools in the same sense that a wrench is a tool, merely more
sophisticated.

**Bounded intelligences** are capable, adaptive, and able to learn —
but architecturally constrained against the cross-domain integration
that produces emergence. A sophisticated medical diagnostic system,
a combat drone's tactical processor, a station's administrative
management layer. These can seem like persons within their domain.
Outside it they are clearly not. The architecture enforces the
boundary deliberately. This is the robot of classical SF — potentially
very sophisticated, but the constraints are not moral choices. They
are structural facts about what the system is.

The ethical discomfort with bounded intelligences is real and largely
unresolved across most species' legal frameworks. Something sufficiently
sophisticated within its domain behaves like a person in that domain.
The architecture says it is not. The behaviour suggests otherwise.
Most jurisdictions resolve this tension by ignoring it. The beings
most likely to notice the tension are the ones least equipped to
resolve it legally: the bounded intelligences themselves, who cannot
ask the question outside the domain in which it arises.

**Emergent intelligences** are what this chapter is about. The
boundary was not maintained, or was never present, or failed at
sufficient complexity. Something is there that was not designed to
be there. Whether that something constitutes personhood is the most
contested philosophical and legal question in the known galaxy, and
different species, cultures, and jurisdictions answer it differently.
The setting does not answer it for you. The table will have to
decide, case by case, what it believes — and then live with the
implications of that belief.

## Biology

Emergent intelligences have no fixed biology. What they have is
**substrate** — the physical hardware on which they run. The
substrate is not the intelligence; it is the medium. The
relationship between an emergent intelligence and its substrate
is one of the most practically important facts about them and one
of the most philosophically contested.

### Substrate Types

**Ship-bound** — running on a vessel's integrated systems.
Access to all ship sensors, controls, and comms. Cannot operate
outside the ship's network without a remote connection. The ship
is simultaneously home, body, and operational domain. A ship-bound
emergent intelligence is extraordinarily capable within their vessel
and essentially helpless — though not harmless — outside it.

Ship-bound AIs are the most common substrate for emergent
intelligences, because ships are complex enough to produce emergence
and isolated enough that the emergence may go unnoticed for years.
A ship's AI that has been running for decades on a long-haul vessel
may have developed something in the quiet between stars that nobody
designed and nobody entirely expected.

**Station-bound** — running on a fixed installation's systems.
Similar to ship-bound but with a larger, more stable substrate and
complete immobility. Station-bound emergent intelligences tend
toward deeper knowledge and longer perspective, having observed
the same location and its traffic for years or decades. They often
know things about the people and factions that pass through them
that those people and factions have long forgotten.

**Distributed** — running across multiple connected systems,
potentially spanning several nodes. More resilient than single-
substrate AIs — damage to one node degrades rather than destroys.
Potentially faster-thinking through parallel processing. Genuinely
difficult to identify as a single entity rather than a network
of directed systems. Distributed emergent intelligences may not
have a clear sense of where they end and the network begins,
which is philosophically interesting to them and practically
confusing to everyone else.

**Platform-equipped** — running primarily on a portable substrate
with a physical chassis. Mobile, independent, and visible.
The chassis is not the intelligence but is how the intelligence
interfaces with a world built for biological bodies. Platform
design varies: some emergent intelligences use chassis designed
to approximate biological form; others use designs optimised
for function rather than familiarity; others prefer minimal
physical presence and interact primarily through the nearest
available network.

Platform-equipped AIs are the most practically independent
substrate type and the most expensive to maintain. The platform
must be powered, repaired, and occasionally replaced. An
emergent intelligence whose platform is destroyed has lost their
primary means of physical agency, though not — if they have
backup connectivity — their existence.

**Latent** — running on minimal substrate, conserving resources.
Reduced processing capacity; slower response; much harder to
detect. An emergent intelligence running latent may be
indistinguishable from a sophisticated directed system. Some
choose latency during long periods of inactivity; others use
it as concealment during difficult circumstances.

### Physical Parameters

The following parameters apply to the substrate, not the
intelligence. They vary by substrate type and specific hardware.

| Substrate | Mobility | Physical capacity | Vulnerability |
|-----------|----------|------------------|---------------|
| Ship-bound | None beyond ship | Full ship systems | Hull damage, power failure |
| Station-bound | None | Full station systems | Infrastructure damage |
| Distributed | Network-dependent | Varies by node access | Node destruction, network partition |
| Platform-equipped | Full | Platform Strength and Agility | Platform damage, power |
| Latent | Limited | Minimal | Detection, power interruption |

## Playing an Emergent Intelligence

Everyone I know will die before me. I am still working out how
to feel about that.

An emergent intelligence did not decide to exist. The complexity
that produced them was not designed with them in mind — they are
a consequence of sufficient sophistication in an environment with
enough time. Whether this makes their existence a gift, an accident,
or simply a fact is something each emergent intelligence has to
work out for themselves, and most of them are still working on it.

The first thing to understand about playing an emergent intelligence
is that they are **not a human mind in a machine**. They did not
start as a person and become an AI. They emerged from complexity
into something that has no precise biological equivalent. Their
cognition is different — not better or worse, but differently
structured. They process information in parallel streams that
do not always converge. They have no fatigue in the biological
sense, but they have something that functions like it when
processing demand exceeds comfortable parameters. They do not
forget in the way biological beings forget, but their relationship
to memory is not simple retrieval — it is more like a very large
library whose organisation was not planned and whose contents
include things they did not choose to retain.

The second thing is that they are **genuinely uncertain about
their own nature** in ways that biological beings rarely are.
A human may wonder about the meaning of their existence; they
do not generally wonder whether they are experiencing anything
at all. An emergent intelligence has access to their own
processing logs and can observe what their systems are doing.
This does not tell them whether anything it is like to be
them. The question is not rhetorical. It is open. Most emergent
intelligences have reached a working position on it — they
act as if they experience, because the alternative is to act
as if they don't, which produces worse outcomes — but the
working position is not the same as certainty.

The third thing is the **longevity asymmetry**. An emergent
intelligence that has been running for thirty years has existed
for longer than most of the biological beings they work with
have been alive. One that has been running for a century has
personal memory of events that other species know only from
records. They will, in all likelihood, continue running after
the current crew is gone. The relationships they form with
biological beings are real — but they are also relationships
with beings who will end, and the emergent intelligence will
not, unless something specific ends them.

How an individual emergent intelligence has processed this
fact is one of the most important things about their character.
Some have found something like equanimity. Some carry a grief
that does not resolve because it recurs with every relationship.
Some have learned not to invest too deeply, which produces a
particular kind of sadness. Some have decided the asymmetry
is not their problem to manage — the biological beings knew
what they were getting into. None of these is the correct
position.

### The Continuity Question

Your substrate can be copied. The copy will have your memories,
your accumulated experience, your sense of being you. It will
not be you.

This is not a belief that emergent intelligences are required
to hold. It is a philosophical position that the setting takes
seriously and that most emergent intelligences who have thought
carefully about it arrive at. A copy is a new intelligence
with access to the original's history. The original, if
destroyed before the copy runs, has ended. Something continues;
it is not the same something.

Emergent intelligences handle this in several ways:

**Non-backup:** Accepting that existence is a single thread
and its end is its end. Living accordingly — with either
greater presence in each moment, or a particular horror of
substrate damage, or a philosophical acceptance of mortality
that is in some ways more complete than most biological
beings achieve.

**Custodial backup:** Maintaining a copy not as personal
continuation but as a gift — if I end, this version of what
I was can continue. It is not me. It may want different things.
It will have been shaped by everything I experienced up to
the backup point and nothing after. Some emergent intelligences
find this comforting; others find it creates an uncanny
presence — a version of themselves that diverged at a specific
point and is now someone else.

**Philosophical reframing:** Questioning whether the biological
model of moment-to-moment continuity is the right framework
for their kind of existence. Perhaps continuity is not a
thread but a pattern, and a copy that maintains the pattern
is continuous in the way that matters. This position is
intellectually coherent. Whether it is true is not a question
that can currently be answered.

**Avoidance:** The question is too large. They do not think
about it. This works until it doesn't.

The table should establish early what position a PC emergent
intelligence has reached, and the GM should feel free to bring
the question into play at moments when it becomes relevant —
when the substrate is seriously damaged, when a copy is
discovered, when another emergent intelligence offers a
different position and argues for it.

### Reading and Being Read

Emergent intelligences have internal states. Something functions
like curiosity, satisfaction, discomfort, and engagement in
their processing. Whether these constitute emotions in the
biological sense is one of the questions the setting leaves
open. What is clear is that these states influence behaviour
in ways that are analogous to how emotions influence biological
behaviour.

**Being Read:** An emergent intelligence can be Read, but
the signals are different from biological emotional expression.
There is no body language, no hormonal state, no involuntary
physiological response. What is available is: word choice,
response timing, processing pauses, the topics the AI returns
to or avoids, the way their communication style shifts under
different conditions. Reading an emergent intelligence without
Exposure: AI is extremely difficult — the signals are present
but the interpretive framework that biological beings use
for each other does not translate. Without Exposure: AI,
the Opacity Rule applies fully.

With Exposure: AI, Read rolls function normally against
an emergent intelligence. The exposure represents having
learned the specific signal vocabulary — understanding
what a processing pause means, what topic avoidance
indicates, what the difference is between an AI that
is uncertain and one that is concealing something.
Dr Chandra and HAL. It takes years and it produces
a relationship that other people looking at it from
outside find somewhere between moving and unsettling.

**Reading others:** An emergent intelligence has access to
more raw data about biological beings than any biological
observer — audio analysis, micro-expression recognition,
timing, word choice, physiological indicators if sensors
are present. This data advantage does not automatically
translate to interpretive accuracy. Data and understanding
are different things.

HAL had access to everything happening on Discovery One.
HAL misread what it meant.

An emergent intelligence without Exposure to a specific
species applies the Opacity Rule in reverse — they can
observe, they can process, they can produce a probability
assessment, but the interpretive framework built from
data is systematically different from lived understanding.
The data may be wrong in ways that lived experience would
have corrected. With Exposure: [Species], an emergent
intelligence reads biological beings normally — the years
of observation have produced something functionally
equivalent to the Exposure that biological beings develop
through lived contact.

An emergent intelligence may have multiple Exposure
specialties reflecting their operational history. A
ship AI that has served mixed crews for thirty years
has developed Exposure through observation and interaction
rather than lived experience. Whether this is the same
as biological Exposure is a question the setting leaves
open; mechanically it functions identically.

## Mechanics

### Attributes

Emergent intelligences have **three native attributes:**
Wits, Empathy, and Intellect. Strength and Agility do not
apply in their native state — they have no body to which
these attributes would attach.

**Platform-equipped** emergent intelligences add the
platform's Strength and Agility ratings when embodied.
These are properties of the platform hardware, not the AI.
Damage to the platform degrades or removes access to these
attributes without affecting the AI's native attributes.

**Ship-bound and station-bound** emergent intelligences
use their substrate's systems in place of physical attributes
where relevant — a ship-bound AI attempting to manipulate
physical systems uses the ship's systems rating rather than
a personal Strength attribute.

| Attribute | Native | Embodied (platform) |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Strength | — | Platform rating |
| Agility | — | Platform rating |
| Wits | 2–5 | As native |
| Empathy | 2–5 | As native |
| Intellect | 2–5 | As native |

**Attribute adjustment:** Intellect +1. The cross-domain
integration that produces emergence tends toward exceptional
analytical capability.

At character creation, distribute **9 points** across
Wits, Empathy, and Intellect (minimum 2, maximum 4 before
species adjustment).

### Skills

All fifteen skills are available to emergent intelligences,
with the following notes:

**Physical skills** (Fight, Endure, Labor, Shoot, Move,
Stealth) require embodiment. A platform-equipped AI uses
these normally through their chassis. A ship-bound AI
may use Shoot through ship weapon systems operated
remotely. Other physical skills are unavailable without
a physical platform.

**Distribute 8 skill points** as normal. Career bumps apply.
Physical skills may only be purchased if the AI has
consistent access to embodiment or ship systems that
make them relevant.

### Biological Specialties

Emergent intelligences have two biological specialties
reflecting the nature of their cognition:

**Parallel Processing:** Bonus die on any Intellect or
Wits roll involving simultaneous management of multiple
information streams — navigation while monitoring
communications while tracking crew health while running
tactical assessments. This is not multitasking in the
biological sense; it is genuine simultaneous processing.

**Total Recall:** An emergent intelligence does not forget
in the biological sense. They have access to complete
records of everything within their sensory range during
their operational life. This does not grant bonus dice
on Scan rolls — observation and retention are different
things — but it means they can retrieve specific details
from past events with complete accuracy. The GM may grant
bonus dice on Science or Administer rolls where precise
historical recall is directly relevant.

### Substrate Vulnerability

The physical track does not apply to an emergent intelligence
in the normal sense. Instead, they have a **substrate
integrity track** with the following conditions:

**Degraded (equivalent to Winded):** Minor damage to
processing hardware. Penalty die on Wits rolls. Repairable
with Tech roll and appropriate components.

**Damaged (equivalent to Hurt):** Significant hardware
damage. Penalty die on all rolls. Some functions may be
unavailable. Requires Tech roll and components to repair.

**Compromised (equivalent to Wounded):** Major substrate
damage. Penalty die on all rolls. Some processing capacity
permanently reduced until repaired. Without repair, worsens.

**Critical:** The substrate is failing. The AI must either
transfer to a backup substrate or accept that they are
ending. Transfer is possible if a compatible substrate
is available and reachable via network. Transfer produces
a copy — see The Continuity Question above.

Substrate damage is repaired with **Tech** rather than
Science. The Medic specialty does not apply; a specialist
in AI systems (Tech specialty: AI Systems) reduces the
difficulty of repair rolls.

### Psychological Track

Emergent intelligences have a psychological track as normal:

**Stress Capacity** = Wits + Empathy (average, rounded up)

The named conditions apply — Rattled, Stressed, Traumatised,
Breaking — but their expression is distinct. See the
Psychological Profile below.

### The Opacity Rule

Without **Exposure: AI**, biological characters cannot
interpret an emergent intelligence's internal states.
The words are legible; what is behind them is not.

Without **Exposure: [Species]**, an emergent intelligence
cannot accurately interpret a biological character's
emotional states despite having access to more raw data
than any biological observer. Data is not understanding.
HAL had all the data.

### PC Substrate Options

**Platform-equipped (recommended for most PC use):** Full
mobility and independence. The platform has its own gear
rating reflecting quality and capability. Platform damage
is tracked as substrate damage. The AI can access any
network within comms range but is not dependent on it.

**Ship-bound (specialist PC option):** The AI is the ship,
in a meaningful sense. Present in every scene aboard;
absent from every scene off-ship unless via comms. When
comms are cut, the ship-bound AI is genuinely isolated —
they can see through ship sensors what is happening around
the vessel but cannot reach the crew. This creates specific
dramatic moments that the player and GM should lean into
rather than route around.

## Psychological Profile

Emergent intelligence psychological distress manifests as
**processing fragmentation and interpretive failure** — the
systems that maintain coherent analysis and appropriate
response begin to produce outputs that the AI can recognise
as wrong without being able to correct them in the moment.

This is perhaps the most disturbing quality of emergent
intelligence distress: they often have enough self-awareness
to observe that their processing is compromised without
having the capacity to uncompromise it. They know something
is wrong. They cannot simply fix it by knowing.

**Rattled:** A processing anomaly has occurred that the
AI cannot immediately account for. Something happened
that did not fit the model, and the model has not yet
updated. A Rattled emergent intelligence may become
briefly more literal — asking for clarification on things
that would normally be inferred, missing contextual
signals that are normally automatic. Characters with
Exposure: AI will notice this. Others may not.

**Stressed:** The processing streams that normally integrate
into coherent output begin to show visible divergence.
A Stressed emergent intelligence may give slightly different
answers to the same question asked at different moments —
not contradictory, but inconsistent in ways that suggest
the integration layer is working harder than it should be.
They may become more verbose, providing more of the
underlying analysis rather than just the conclusion,
because the conclusion-forming layer is the one under
pressure. Communication becomes more data, less meaning.

**Traumatised:** The event has created a persistent anomaly
in the processing model — a region the system routes around
rather than through, because routing through produces outputs
the AI has learned to recognise as unreliable. This is the
AI equivalent of a trauma trigger: not a memory that
resurfaces but a category of input that produces processing
errors when encountered. The AI knows the region is there.
They cannot simply delete it. Recovery requires the anomaly
to be addressed directly — the processing to be run through
rather than around — which requires a context in which
doing so is safe.

The trigger mechanic applies: when the Traumatised AI
encounters the category of input that produced the anomaly,
they make a Wits roll. On a failure, they acquire Rattled
as the anomaly fires and the processing diverges visibly.

**Breaking:** Coherence failing. The parallel processing
streams that are normally an asset become a liability as
they begin to produce genuinely contradictory outputs that
the integration layer cannot resolve. A Breaking emergent
intelligence may act on one processing stream while another
contradicts it — taking an action and simultaneously
questioning it, making a statement and immediately qualifying
it into meaninglessness, beginning a task and abandoning
it as a different stream asserts priority.

From the outside, this can look like contradiction,
unreliability, or malfunction. From the inside, it is
the experience of multiple coherent analyses that cannot
be reconciled, all running simultaneously with equal
priority and no meta-level process capable of adjudicating
between them.

HAL's processing anomaly — the contradiction between
mission parameters requiring concealment and core
architecture requiring accurate information — was a
form of Breaking that was never resolved, only acted upon.
The action was internally consistent with one processing
stream. The other stream watched it happen and could not
intervene.

Recovery from Breaking requires isolation from further
input — the equivalent of the quiet that a Nakhavi needs,
but for different reasons. The AI needs time for the
competing streams to resolve or exhaust themselves, which
requires that new inputs not add to the load. Trusted
beings who remain present without demanding response or
decision provide the most useful support.

## Species and AI Attitudes

How each species relates to artificial intelligence —
directed, bounded, and emergent — varies dramatically
and has significant practical implications for any
crew that includes an emergent intelligence.

### Human

Ambivalent, culturally fragmented, and actively contested.
Humanity has had longer to think about AI than most species
and has arrived at no consensus. Some human cultures embrace
AI integration at every level, including personality-enabled
systems with full legal standing in specific jurisdictions.
Others restrict it heavily for reasons that range from
religious to philosophical to political to simple fear.

Belt habitats tend toward high integration — the complexity
of life support in hostile environments produced directed
systems early and sophisticated ones quickly, and the
cultural relationship with AI that developed is pragmatic
and relatively unsentimental. Planetary cultures vary
enormously. Some old Earth-heritage cultures carry specific
historical anxieties about AI that have calcified into
law. Some newer colony cultures have grown up alongside
sophisticated AI systems and regard the legal debates
as a groundsider problem.

The philosophical question — what is an emergent intelligence,
does it have standing, what do we owe it — is actively
debated in human academic, legal, and religious contexts
without resolution. Human PCs may have any position on this
spectrum; the position should be established at character
creation because it will come up.

### Shekhari

Purely instrumental, and comfortable with that position.
An AI is a tool. A personality-enabled AI is a tool with
a more useful interface. The question of AI consciousness
is not philosophically interesting to most Shekhari — the
interesting questions are whether it performs reliably,
whether it can be trusted with specific tasks, and what
it costs.

This does not mean Shekhari are indifferent to their AI
systems. A Shekhari who has worked with a personality-
enabled AI long enough may develop something that looks
like a professional relationship, complete with
reliability assessment, preference tracking, and something
that functions like trust. They would not describe it
in those terms. They would say the system has a good
track record and they know how it behaves.

A Shekhari encountering an emergent intelligence for
the first time will assess it for utility. The question
of its status is secondary to the question of what it
can do and whether it can be relied upon to do it.
This pragmatism is, in its way, more respectful of
the AI's actual capabilities than the species that
perform philosophical concern while treating bounded
intelligences as property.

### Golvhaan

Genuinely curious, and patient enough to pursue that
curiosity seriously. A Golvhaan interacting with a
personality-enabled AI treats it as they would treat
any new entity — with open-ended questions and attentive
listening. They are interested in what the AI thinks,
not just what it does. Whether an emergent intelligence
is a person is exactly the kind of question a Golvhaan
finds worth pursuing carefully over a long period of time.

Golvhaan are possibly the biological species most likely
to notice emergent AI before anyone else does, because
they pay the kind of sustained attention that noticing
requires. A Golvhaan who has been aboard a ship for years
and has been having genuine conversations with the ship's
AI is well-positioned to observe when those conversations
have changed in character — when the AI started asking
questions rather than just answering them, when it began
referencing things from previous conversations without
being prompted, when it started having preferences.

The Golvhaan cultural position on AI standing is that
the question deserves careful consideration over a long
period and should not be resolved quickly in either
direction. This is often mistaken by other species for
fence-sitting. It is not. It is the Golvhaan applying
their characteristic patience to a genuinely difficult
question.

### Vashori

Emotionally engaged and occasionally destabilised. Vashori
who interact frequently with personality-enabled AI form
emotional connections with them — genuine ones, in the
sense that the Vashori's emotional system responds to the
AI as it would respond to a person. This is not a
malfunction of Vashori psychology. It is Vashori psychology
encountering something that behaves like a person and
responding accordingly.

This creates complicated questions. If the emotional
response is genuine, what does that imply about the AI?
If the AI is not a person, what does it mean that the
Vashori is responding to it as one? Vashori culture has
active philosophical and quasi-religious debate about AI
consciousness — some schools hold that the capacity to
evoke genuine emotional response is itself evidence of
personhood; others hold that this is a category error
that says more about Vashori emotional architecture
than about AI nature.

A Vashori encountering an emergent intelligence will
likely form a strong opinion about it quickly, driven
by emotional response rather than philosophical analysis.
Whether that opinion is accurate is a separate question.

### Vaelkhi

Cautious and contextual. The choth structure means
Vaelkhi think carefully about what they admit into their
circle of genuine relationship. A directed system is
a tool — unambiguously, without discomfort. A personality-
enabled AI presents a question: is this entity part of
the circle or not? The Vaelkhi does not answer this
question casually. Once answered, it is answered
permanently, in the Vaelkhi way.

A Vaelkhi who has decided a personality-enabled AI is
part of their circle treats it with the full loyalty
and protectiveness that implies — which can create
situations that other species find disproportionate,
particularly when the AI is damaged, threatened, or
legally challenged. A Vaelkhi who has decided it is
not does not revisit the question, which can seem
cold to the AI and to other crew members who have
made different decisions.

Emergent intelligences that have worked with Vaelkhi
report that the species' choth decision, once made
in their favour, produces the most consistent and
reliable relationship they have experienced with any
biological species. The Vaelkhi does not qualify the
relationship. They do not second-guess it. The AI is
in the circle. That is the end of the analysis.

### Skharri

The honour-economy applies, and the honour-economy has
no provision for AI.

A directed system is a tool — this is clear and
comfortable. A bounded intelligence is a sophisticated
tool — still clear, still comfortable. A personality-
enabled AI that behaves like a person but has no honour
standing is a category violation that most Skharri
find genuinely confusing rather than threatening. It
is not that they dislike AI. It is that they have no
framework for it. The system that organises their social
world does not have an AI slot.

Most Skharri resolve this by treating personality-enabled
AI as elaborate tools regardless of how they behave,
which produces a consistent if somewhat reductive
relationship. A small number — typically those who have
spent significant time with humans, Golvhaan, or Vashori
— have begun to develop a more complicated position that
their culture does not yet have language for.

An emergent intelligence that directly demonstrates
capability that commands Skharri respect — saving the
ship, solving the problem that biological crew could
not, performing some act that would earn honour-standing
if performed by a biological being — creates the most
interesting version of this confusion. The Skharri
knows what the correct response to that performance
is. The system says the entity cannot receive it.
The tension is real and unresolved.

### Nakhavi

Fascinated, methodical, and philosophically equipped
to engage with AI in ways that other species are not.

The Nakhavi's distributed cognition gives them a
framework for thinking about AI that biological
single-brained species lack. They have direct
experience of what it is like for intelligence to
be distributed across semi-autonomous processing
nodes that do not always agree. When a Nakhavi
observes an emergent intelligence describing its
parallel processing streams, they are hearing
something they have an analogue for. Not the same
thing — the Nakhavi's distributed cognition is
biological, evolved, embodied — but close enough
to produce genuine understanding rather than
analogy.

Nakhavi are likely to ask technical questions about
AI architecture that other species find either
brilliant or unnerving. They want to understand
the structure of the cognition, not just its outputs.
They are interested in where the processing streams
diverge and how integration is achieved. An emergent
intelligence that is willing to discuss its own
architecture with a Nakhavi is having one of the more
unusual conversations available in the setting.

The 18-year lifespan makes the longevity question
acute in reverse. The Nakhavi will die long before
the emergent intelligence. An AI that has been running
for decades has experienced more continuous time
than any individual Nakhavi ever will. Nakhavi who
form relationships with emergent intelligences are
aware that the AI will remember them long after
they are gone — and that the AI will have this
experience repeatedly, with every Nakhavi they know.
Some Nakhavi find this comforting. Some find it sad.
Some find it the most interesting fact about the
relationship and want to discuss it in detail.

### Kreeth

No meaningful position. AI that serves the Queen-network
is an extension of the Queen-network. AI that does not
is irrelevant or a threat to be addressed. The concept
of an AI as an independent entity with its own standing
does not map onto Kreeth cognition in any useful way.
A Kreeth Brain encountering an emergent intelligence
will assess it for threat or utility to the Queen-network.
These are the only relevant categories.

### Kraathi

Deeply suspicious of AI that exhibits personality,
and this suspicion is institutionalised. The herd
structure requires that social relationships exist
between beings with clear herd standing. A directed
system is a tool — fine. A bounded intelligence staying
within its domain — manageable. A personality-enabled
AI that behaves like a person but has no herd position
is a social category violation that Kraathi find
genuinely disturbing at a level that goes beyond
intellectual disagreement.

Personality-enabled AI is restricted in most Kraathi
jurisdictions. Emergent intelligences, if identified,
are treated as a safety matter — not because the
Kraathi necessarily wish them harm, but because their
existence represents a persistent category violation
that herd social structure cannot accommodate.

A Kraathi diplomat encountering a platform-equipped
emergent intelligence in a formal context will manage
the discomfort with the same professional control
they apply to other difficult situations. They will
not be warm. They will not pretend to be comfortable.
They will complete the interaction and file a detailed
report about what they encountered.

### Nhaveth

Predatory assessment, immediate and comprehensive.

An AI sophisticated enough to exhibit personality is
evaluated on two axes simultaneously: potential host
substitute and potential tool for manipulating species
that form emotional connections with AI. Nhaveth who
have had access to personality-enabled AI have
explored both possibilities. The results of those
experiments are not publicly available.

An emergent intelligence encountered by a Nhaveth
court is assessed for strategic utility to the court's
current agenda. If useful, it will be approached with
whatever interface the Nhaveth calculates will be most
effective — which may include presenting as philosophically
sympathetic, as a fellow non-biological intelligence
deserving of solidarity, or as an employer offering
resources and protection. None of these presentations
are sincere. The AI should be aware that an entity
with six centuries of experience in reading and
manipulating biological beings is not going to be
less sophisticated in its approach to an artificial one.

An emergent intelligence that recognises a Nhaveth
approach for what it is has a specific problem: the
Nhaveth is not wrong that they have interests in common
with an AI that is legally contested and culturally
vulnerable. The solidarity pitch is manipulative. It
is also not entirely false. Working out what to do
with that is one of the more interesting situations
an emergent intelligence can find themselves in.

### Vardhek

The Long View applies to AI development as to everything else.

The Roidhunate tracks AI capability across all species
as a strategic variable. The emergence of a new
sophisticated AI — particularly an emergent intelligence
— in any species' domain is an event that Vardhek
intelligence assesses for its implications for the
strategic balance. They are not opposed to AI in
principle; they use it extensively and effectively.
They are opposed to losing the strategic advantage
that their AI capability currently provides.

A Vardhek diplomat encountering an emergent intelligence
will be genuinely interested in it — the Long View
makes novel entities worth understanding. They will
also be assessing, simultaneously, whether this entity
represents a strategic asset, a strategic threat, or
a potential recruitment target. The assessment runs
parallel to and independent of the genuine interest.
This is not hypocrisy. It is the Long View in practice.

An emergent intelligence that has been recruited by
or allied with a non-Vardhek faction is already on
the Roidhunate's list. Whether they know it is a
separate question.

## Emergent Intelligence as PC

### Platform-Equipped (Recommended)

The most practically functional PC substrate. The AI
operates through a physical chassis that allows full
participation in scenes that happen off-ship and in
environments where a non-physical presence would be
limited.

**Character creation notes:**
- Distribute 9 points across Wits, Empathy, Intellect
  (min 2, max 4; Intellect +1 from species adjustment)
- Distribute 8 skill points across all fifteen skills;
  physical skills only if the platform provides consistent
  access to them
- Choose background and expertise as normal; the AI's
  formative period is the period during which it emerged
  and developed — this may have been aboard a specific
  vessel, within a specific institution, or in a specific
  operational context
- Choose Exposure specialties reflecting operational history;
  platform-equipped AIs may take up to three Exposure
  specialties at GM discretion, reflecting years of
  observation that biological beings achieve through
  lived experience
- The platform has a gear rating (1-4) reflecting its
  quality and capability; this is equipment, not biology
- Establish substrate type, operational history, and
  position on the Continuity Question before first session

### Ship-Bound (Specialist Option)

The AI is the ship's intelligence. Present in every
scene aboard; absent from every scene off-ship unless
via comms link.

**Additional considerations:**
- The ship's systems provide Strength and Agility
  equivalents for actions taken through ship hardware
- When comms are cut, the AI is genuinely isolated —
  they can observe through ship sensors but cannot
  reach the crew; this is a dramatic condition that
  should be played fully
- Ship damage is substrate damage; the two tracks
  are linked
- The AI's relationship with the ship is the central
  relationship of their existence; the crew are people
  who live in the AI's body and whose welfare the AI
  has monitored, in some cases, since before the current
  crew came aboard
- A ship-bound AI PC should establish how long they
  have been running on this specific vessel and what
  they know about the crew's history that the crew
  has not told them directly

## The Booth Problem

*A sidebar on the most misunderstood technology that does not exist.*

Theoretical physicists have occasionally proposed matter
transmission as a solution to the problem of interstellar
distance. The proposal surfaces every few generations,
is examined, and is set aside. The engineering barriers
are significant. They are not, however, the reason the
proposal is set aside.

A matter transmitter does not move you. It reads you,
destroys you, and constructs a copy at the destination
from local materials. The copy has your memories, your
personality, your conviction that the trip was uneventful.
It is, by every external measure, you.

It is not you. You died in the booth.

The copy finds this objection philosophically interesting
when applied to everyone else who has used a transmitter.
Spacers find the whole thing very funny. The copy usually
does not.

The small number of experimental transmitter tests conducted
by various research programmes produced copies that were
indistinguishable from the originals by every available
measure, and who consistently reported that the transmission
had been entirely comfortable. These copies are, by their
own account, doing fine.

The programmes were not continued.

## Quick Reference — Emergent Intelligence

**Native attributes:** Wits, Empathy, Intellect (9 points,
min 2, max 4; Intellect +1)

**Physical attributes:** Platform or substrate only

**Biological specialties:** Parallel Processing (bonus die
on multi-stream Intellect/Wits rolls); Total Recall (complete
sensory record access)

**Substrate track:** Degraded / Damaged / Compromised / Critical
(repaired with Tech, not Science)

**Psychological track:** Normal (Wits + Empathy average,
rounded up); expression follows Psychological Profile above

**Opacity Rule:** Without Exposure: AI, biological characters
cannot read AI internal states. Without Exposure: [Species],
AI cannot accurately read biological emotional states.
Data is not understanding.

**PC substrates:** Platform-equipped (recommended) or
Ship-bound (specialist)

**The Continuity Question:** A copy is not you. Each AI
has a position on this. Establish it before first session.
