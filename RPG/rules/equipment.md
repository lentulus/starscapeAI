# Personal Equipment

## How to Use This Chapter

Equipment entries describe what a piece of gear does, what it
costs in dice pool terms, and what it means to own and use it.
They do not describe every variant, every manufacturer, or every
modification. The examples here are illustrations of the framework;
the framework is the thing.

### Reading an Equipment Entry

Each entry includes:

**Gear rating** — the number of green dice the equipment adds
to the relevant roll. Reflects both hardware quality and the
sophistication of the AI systems embedded in it. A higher gear
rating is not always better; it is more capable, which sometimes
means more demanding.

**Tech level** — which stage of development the equipment
represents (Early / Developed / Advanced / Frontier). Determines
availability relative to local infrastructure and the character
of the AI embedded in the system. See the AI and Technology
chapter.

**Optimal use** — the conditions under which the equipment
performs at its rated gear value. Outside these conditions,
the rating may be reduced or a penalty die applied.

**Notes** — anything that matters about this piece of equipment
that the numbers do not capture.

### Availability and Price

Equipment availability and price are not fixed values. They
are a function of **local species presence and industrial
infrastructure**.

Near the industrial base of the species that manufactures
a piece of equipment: standard availability, standard price,
local expertise for maintenance and repair.

Away from that industrial base: special order, premium price,
longer lead time, and local expertise that may be limited or
absent. A Golvhaan vacc suit three jumps from the nearest
Golvhaan industrial world is not impossible to obtain; it is
expensive, slow to arrive, and the port technician who services
it may be working from a manual they have never had to use before.

The GM applies this principle to all equipment purchases. Players
who pay attention to the setting's demographic texture — who is
present where, in what numbers, with what infrastructure — will
find this rule rewards that attention. A free trader running
routes between a species' industrial base and frontier regions
where that species is settling has a natural cargo.

### Creating New Equipment

When a player wants a piece of equipment not in this chapter,
use this framework:

**What does it do?** State the fictional function clearly.
If it cannot be stated clearly, the equipment is not ready
to be designed.

**Which skill does it support?** Equipment adds gear dice
to a specific skill roll or category of rolls. If it supports
multiple skills, it probably should not — or the dice bonus
should be lower for each application.

**What tech level is it?** Early, Developed, Advanced, or
Frontier. This determines the AI character of the embedded
systems, the installation or acquisition requirements, and
the availability rule above.

**What is the gear rating?** 1-4, matching the tech level.
If the equipment seems like it should be Gear 5, it is either
not a personal equipment item or it is a plot device rather
than standard kit.

**What are the constraints?** Every piece of equipment has
optimal conditions and suboptimal ones. What reduces the gear
rating or adds a penalty die? Power requirements, environmental
limits, species compatibility, maintenance demands.

**What does it mean to own it?** The question that separates
interesting equipment from a stat block. A Gear 4 scope has
a relationship with the shooter. A personal medical kit has
a history. A vacc suit has saved someone's life, or it hasn't
yet. Equipment that has a story is equipment that matters at
the table.

## Weapons — Melee

Melee weapons are used with the **Fight** skill. They are
effective only at Close range. Natural weapons (claws, teeth,
mass) are always available and treat as Gear 1 for species
that have them — see the Species chapter.

### A Note on Melee in This Setting

Melee combat in an interstellar setting is not the primary
mode of violence. It is, however, common in specific contexts:
boarding actions, confined spaces where ranged fire risks hull
integrity, honour duelling cultures, and situations where the
people involved are too close for ranged weapons to be relevant.
A character with high Fight is not anachronistic — they are
someone who has thought carefully about what happens when the
range closes.

---

### Blade (various)
**Gear rating:** 1-2 | **Tech level:** Early to Developed

The oldest personal weapon. In the 22nd century and beyond,
blades exist in forms ranging from simple cutting edges (Gear 1)
to monomolecular-edge tools with embedded impact sensors
and grip-pressure feedback (Gear 2). The Gear 2 blade is not
dramatically different in appearance from the Gear 1 version;
the difference is in edge retention, balance, and the micro-
adjustment systems that keep the edge aligned under impact stress.

**Optimal use:** Close range, all environments including vacuum
(no moving parts to freeze or jam).

**Notes:** Blades are cross-species in a way that many weapons
are not. Something with an edge works regardless of the user's
body plan, though the specific form factor varies enormously —
a blade designed for Skharri grip geometry is different from one
designed for human hands. Near the relevant species' industrial
base, species-specific blades are available. Away from it, a
blade designed for a different hand is a penalty die on Fight.

> *Skharri honour blades are a specific category. The design
> is ancient and has not changed in centuries — to modify it
> would be to change what it is. Gear 1 by any technical
> assessment. Worth more than the rating suggests.*

---

### Monoblade
**Gear rating:** 2 | **Tech level:** Developed

A blade whose edge is maintained at the molecular level by
an embedded directed system. Cuts through materials that would
stop a conventional blade. The directed system requires power
and will shut down at low charge, reverting to a conventional
but well-made blade. Maintenance requires Tech skill and
appropriate equipment; the edge system is fragile in ways
that conventional blades are not.

**Optimal use:** Close range, targets wearing armour or with
significant natural protection.

**Notes:** A monoblade through vacc suit material is a crisis.
Users in pressurised environments think carefully about this.

---

### Impact Weapon (various)
**Gear rating:** 1 | **Tech level:** Early

Clubs, batons, wrenches used as weapons, the business end of
a tool applied to a person. Universally available because the
category encompasses anything heavy. The only weapon available
in every environment regardless of local species presence.

**Optimal use:** Close range. Effective against unarmoured targets.

**Notes:** Impact weapons used for subduing — the explicit
intent not to kill — remove the penalty die on subduing attacks
that blades impose. Blunt force is better calibrated for
incapacitation than edged weapons.

---

### Combat Gauntlet / Augmented Strike
**Gear rating:** 2 | **Tech level:** Developed

Powered strike-enhancement systems built into gloves, boots,
or equivalent appendage covering. Multiplies impact force
through brief mechanical assistance timed to the strike.
The AI times the assistance to the user's strike pattern;
a system that has worked with the same user for years has
a better model of their fighting style than one fresh from
the shop.

**Optimal use:** Close range, unarmed fighting style.

**Notes:** Compatible with most bilateral species. Radically
different design required for Golvhaan, Vaelkhi (talon-mount),
and Nakhavi (arm-mount). Availability follows species presence rule.

---

## Weapons — Ranged

Ranged weapons are used with the **Shoot** skill. Each weapon
has an optimal range band — Close, Short, or Long — at which
it performs at full gear rating. Outside the optimal band,
apply a penalty die.

### Kinetic Weapons

Kinetic weapons propel a physical projectile. They do not
require a power source for the basic firing mechanism (though
advanced variants may use power for targeting systems). They
are reliable in conditions that degrade energy weapons, and
they do not have the energy weapon's characteristic of making
the target location obvious to anyone with appropriate sensors.

---

### Sidearm
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Early to Advanced
**Optimal range:** Short

The standard personal weapon. Available in every spaceport
in the known galaxy in some form. The gear rating reflects
the targeting AI and ammunition management system rather than
the basic mechanism, which is similar across all levels.

**Gear 1:** Manual sighting. Basic recoil compensation.
Fires when you pull the trigger.

**Gear 2:** Integrated targeting display. Automatic range
compensation. Basic threat-profile recognition — the AI
will flag that you are pointing the weapon at a crew member
whose biometrics it has on file. You can still fire. It
will note that you did.

**Gear 3:** Full environmental ballistic compensation.
Target physiological assessment if sensor data is available.
Firing window optimisation. The AI has a model of your
shooting patterns and is quietly adjusting for your known
tendencies. It does not discuss this unless asked.

**Notes:** Sidearms designed for one species' hand geometry
take a penalty die when used by a significantly different
species. Shekhari sidearms look like toys to humans and
are not — the calibre is smaller but the targeting AI
compensates for the reduced mass with improved placement
accuracy. A Gear 3 Shekhari sidearm in human hands is a
Gear 1 weapon and vice versa without modification.

---

### Combat Rifle
**Gear rating:** 2-4 | **Tech level:** Developed to Frontier
**Optimal range:** Long

The standard long-range personal weapon. Requires two hands
or equivalent in standard configuration. The AI embedded
in a combat rifle at Gear 3-4 is doing a significant portion
of the work that a skilled shooter would do manually at
lower gear ratings.

**Gear 2:** Stabilised optics, basic environmental compensation,
burst-fire management. The shooter makes the calls; the rifle
executes them more precisely.

**Gear 3:** Full ballistic AI with real-time environmental
modelling. Optimal firing window display. Target movement
prediction. The rifle and the shooter are working together.

**Gear 4:** See the sniper scope entry below applied to a
full weapon system. The rifle has a model of you, a model
of the environment, and a model of the target. It is making
recommendations that you would not have made without it.
On difficult shots in poor conditions, it will suggest
waiting. Users who have worked with the same Gear 4 weapon
for years report that it has developed preferences about
engagement ranges and approaches. Manufacturers note that
this is a feature of the targeting AI's model refinement,
not a personality. Some users are not certain the distinction
matters.

**Notes:** At Close range, a combat rifle takes a penalty die —
it is too long for confined spaces. At Short range, no penalty.

---

### Sniper System
**Gear rating:** 2-4 | **Tech level:** Developed to Frontier
**Optimal range:** Long (extended)

A precision long-range weapon system. The distinction from
a standard combat rifle is not primarily the mechanism but
the optics, the AI, and the calibration. A sniper system
is designed for one shot at a specific target under specific
conditions. It is not a combat weapon in the conventional
sense; it is a precision instrument.

**Gear 2:** Extended-range optics with basic environmental
compensation. The shooter has the skills; the system
supports them.

**Gear 3:** Real-time atmospheric analysis, target movement
prediction, micro-adjustment of the reticle position, optimal
firing window calculation. The system conducts an analysis
of the shot environment that would take a Gear 1 practitioner
significant time and specialised tools. It conducts it in
real time, continuously, and updates as conditions change.

**Gear 4:** Everything above, plus a biometric model of
the shooter — fatigue, stress indicators, fine motor tremor
— incorporated into the firing window assessment. The system
has developed, through operational use, a model of this
specific shooter that it applies without being asked. When
conditions are wrong, it will indicate that the shot should
wait. Not recommend. Indicate. Some shooters find this
reassuring. Some find it presumptuous. The system does not
adjust its behaviour based on which reaction the shooter has,
but it notes the reaction.

**Notes:** A sniper system is not cross-species. The calibration
is specific to the shooter's physiology, body position, and
shooting patterns. A Gear 3 sniper system calibrated for a
human shooter in the hands of a Vaelkhi is a Gear 1 weapon.
Recalibration requires a Tech roll and several hours. A
Gear 4 system will begin recalibrating itself when it detects
that the user's biometrics do not match its stored model,
which is either very convenient or slightly alarming depending
on how you feel about your weapon having opinions about who
is using it.

---

### Shotgun / Close-Range Weapon
**Gear rating:** 1-2 | **Tech level:** Early to Developed
**Optimal range:** Close to Short

Wide-pattern kinetic weapons designed for short-range
effectiveness. Less precise than rifles; more forgiving
of targeting error. The AI at Gear 2 manages spread pattern
and can tighten or widen it based on the target environment.

**Notes:** In confined spaces — ship corridors, station
interiors — shotguns are dangerous to users as well as
targets. Hull-safe ammunition exists (Gear 2 variant)
and is standard in shipboard contexts. Using standard
ammunition in a pressurised hull is the kind of decision
that ends careers and sometimes lives.

---

### Crew-Served Weapon
**Gear rating:** 3 | **Tech level:** Advanced
**Optimal range:** Long

Heavy weapons requiring two operators or a mount: tripod-
mounted automatic weapons, portable missile systems, area-
denial devices. The AI at this level manages ammunition,
targeting, and threat prioritisation across the weapon's
field of engagement. One operator can manage the weapon
alone with a penalty die; two operators working in coordination
use the Group Action rules.

**Notes:** Crew-served weapons are military equipment and
are regulated or prohibited in most civilian contexts.
The Administer skill and local legal knowledge determine
what carrying one means in a given jurisdiction.

---

### Energy Weapons

Energy weapons direct coherent energy at a target. They
require power — either from a dedicated power cell or
from a connected power source. They have no physical
ammunition to manage, which is an advantage; they are
sensitive to power supply issues, which is a constraint.
At higher tech levels, energy weapons are not necessarily
more powerful than kinetic equivalents — they are more
precise, more controllable, and more capable of effects
that kinetic weapons cannot produce (cutting, welding,
non-lethal stunning).

---

### Laser Weapon
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Early to Advanced
**Optimal range:** Short to Long (varies by power level)

Coherent light as a weapon. The oldest energy weapon type
and still the most common. Reliable, precise, and scalable —
the same basic technology produces a surgical cutting tool
at low power and a combat weapon at high power.

**Gear 1:** Fixed power output, manual targeting. Limited
range. The AI manages power draw and thermal cycling.

**Gear 2:** Variable power output with the AI managing
power allocation in real time. Extended range. Target
acquisition support. Can switch between lethal and
stunning modes; the AI tracks which mode is active and
flags mode changes.

**Gear 3:** Pulse management for maximum range and
penetration. Atmospheric compensation (coherent light
is affected by atmospheric density and composition).
Target material assessment — the AI identifies what
the target is made of and adjusts pulse characteristics
for optimal effect. At this level, a laser weapon in the
hands of a surgeon and in the hands of a soldier is
the same hardware doing very different things, both very well.

**Notes:** Laser weapons are visible — the beam is the
weapon, and in certain atmospheric conditions it is visible
to the naked eye. Energy discharge produces a detectable
sensor signature. Neither of these properties is present
in kinetic weapons. In contexts where discretion matters,
this is a significant consideration.

---

### Plasma Weapon
**Gear rating:** 3-4 | **Tech level:** Advanced to Frontier
**Optimal range:** Short

Directed ionised gas at temperatures that are destructive
to most materials. More powerful than lasers at equivalent
range; less precise; significantly higher power requirement;
shorter effective range due to plasma dispersal. The AI at
Gear 3-4 manages plasma containment, dispersal rate, and
firing sequence — without competent AI management, plasma
weapons are as dangerous to the user as to the target.

**Notes:** Plasma weapons are restricted in most jurisdictions.
They are effective against armoured targets. They are a poor
choice in pressurised environments for reasons that do not
require explanation. The AI will flag when a firing solution
would compromise hull integrity; whether the user listens
is their decision.

---

### Non-Lethal Options

Most ranged weapons at Gear 2+ have a non-lethal mode.
The AI manages the transition and ensures the power level
is appropriate for incapacitation rather than injury.
Dedicated non-lethal weapons (stunners, sonic disruptors,
chemical delivery systems) are available at Gear 1-2 and
are legal in most jurisdictions where lethal weapons are
not.

---

## Vacc Suits

A vacc suit is not a single piece of equipment. It is a
system: pressure management, atmospheric processing, thermal
regulation, life support, communications, and mobility support,
integrated into a form factor that accommodates the user's
body plan and biology.

The gear rating of a vacc suit applies to **Endure rolls**
made in hostile environments while suited. The suit is not
adding to the user's attack pool; it is adding to their
survival pool.

### The Life Support AI

Every vacc suit at Gear 2 and above has a life support
directed system that monitors suit integrity, atmospheric
levels, thermal state, and the user's biological indicators,
and responds to changes within its operational parameters.
At Gear 3-4, this system is sophisticated enough to provide
medical support — monitoring the user's physiological state,
administering basic medications from an integrated pharmacological
module, and flagging medical conditions to the user or to
external medical systems.

A suit whose life support AI has been working with the same
user for years has a model of that user's normal physiological
parameters that allows it to detect deviations earlier and
more accurately than a system working with an unfamiliar user.
This is not a personality — the life support AI is a directed
system — but it is a form of accumulated knowledge that has
practical value.

### Standard Vacc Suit (Human baseline)
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Early to Advanced

The reference point against which non-human suits are compared.
Available in most spaceports. Maintained by most port technicians.

**Gear 1:** Basic pressure maintenance, oxygen recycling,
thermal insulation. No atmospheric analysis. Manual seal
check. Battery life measured in hours. The suit keeps you
alive; it does not tell you much about what is happening.

**Gear 2:** Integrated atmospheric analysis, thermal management
with active heating and cooling, suit integrity monitoring
with breach detection, external sensor suite for basic
environmental awareness, pharmacological module for emergency
medication. Battery life measured in days. The suit is actively
managing your environment rather than passively maintaining it.

**Gear 3:** Full physiological monitoring, predictive integrity
assessment (flags stress points before they breach), external
sensor suite with AI-assisted analysis, pharmacological module
with a treatment library that covers most human medical
emergencies, and communication suite with encryption. Battery
life measured in weeks on reduced consumption. The suit knows
more about your current physical state than you do, and it is
doing something about it.

**Notes (all ratings):** The suit's mobility systems must
accommodate the user's body plan. A human suit on a human
is rated as above. See non-human variants below.

---

### Non-Human Vacc Suits

Non-human vacc suits follow the same gear rating system and
provide equivalent protection at equivalent ratings. The
differences are in the engineering challenges specific to
each species' biology and the availability rule: suits designed
for a specific species are available at standard price and
lead time near that species' industrial base, and at premium
price with extended lead time elsewhere.

---

#### Shekhari Suit
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Early to Advanced

Human-scale in pressure and atmospheric requirements; radically
different in form factor. The engineering challenges:

**The tail** — a prehensile tail requires a fully articulated,
pressure-sealed joint at the suit's posterior. This is a known
weak point and is the component most commonly requiring
maintenance on Shekhari suits.

**Scale** — the suit must fit a 10kg, 0.55m user. This is
not a problem of miniaturisation (the components scale down
reasonably well) but of precision engineering in a small form
factor that still needs to accommodate all suit functions.

**Arboreal articulation** — the suit must not restrict the
Shekhari's range of movement, which is considerably greater
than a bilateral biped's in three-dimensional environments.
Standard human suit joint design is inadequate.

**Notes:** A Shekhari in a human vacc suit that has been
modified to fit is in a suit that takes a penalty die on
Move rolls and has impaired tail function. A purpose-built
Shekhari suit has no such penalty. The tail joint is the
first thing to inspect during suit maintenance.

---

#### Golvhaan Suit
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Early to Advanced

The most engineering-intensive standard vacc suit in the setting.
The challenges:

**Mass and volume** — 700kg of draconic centauroid requires
a suit of corresponding scale. A Golvhaan suit is not something
that can be improvised or substituted. It is a significant
piece of engineering that requires specialist manufacture.

**Four-limb lower body** — the leg configuration requires
four sealed, articulated pressure joints for the lower limbs
plus two for the upper. Six limb joints rather than the human
suit's four, each of which must maintain pressure at a range
of angles and loads.

**High-gravity physiology** — the suit's structural components
must handle the load-bearing requirements of a 700kg user
in standard gravity. Materials technology matters here; a
Golvhaan suit at Gear 1 in materials technology is heavier
and less mobile than one at Gear 3.

**Metabolic throughput** — the atmospheric recycling system
must handle Golvhaan caloric and oxygen throughput, which
significantly exceeds human equivalents. Battery life is
shorter; oxygen consumption is higher; thermal management
must handle a larger body's heat generation.

**Notes:** A Golvhaan suit is a substantial capital asset.
It is not available improvised, substituted, or modified
from other species' equipment. Crews with Golvhaan members
plan EVA operations around suit availability and suit
maintenance with care. The suit's size means it cannot
be stored in standard suit lockers; ships carrying Golvhaan
crew modify their EVA preparation areas accordingly.

---

#### Vashori Suit
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Early to Advanced

Human-scale bilateral biped in form factor, which makes the
basic suit design manageable. The critical engineering is thermal.

**Thermal management** — a standard human suit's thermal
floor (the minimum temperature the suit maintains) is
insufficient for Vashori cold sensitivity. A Vashori in
a human suit in cold conditions is accumulating a cold
environmental condition that the suit is not compensating
for. Purpose-built Vashori suits have more aggressive
heating, better thermal insulation, and monitoring
calibrated to Vashori physiological parameters rather
than human ones.

**Monitoring calibration** — the life support AI at Gear 2+
must be calibrated to Vashori physiology. Ectothermic
metabolic parameters are different from endothermic ones;
a suit monitoring for human physiological norms will
miss early warning signs in a Vashori user.

**Notes:** A Vashori in a human suit with Gear 2+ systems
can function if the suit's thermal systems are manually
set to maintain higher temperatures. This reduces battery
life. The life support AI's medical monitoring should be
noted as uncalibrated for Vashori users — it will provide
alerts, but they may be delayed or incorrect.

---

#### Vaelkhi Suit
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Early to Advanced

The wing problem defines everything about Vaelkhi EVA equipment.

**Wing accommodation** — a Vaelkhi suit either folds the
wings (applying the corridor constraint mechanic for extended
EVA operations — after one hour, penalty die on all rolls
until 30 minutes of unfolded time) or is built with full
wing articulation. Full-articulation suits are significantly
larger, significantly more expensive, and not useful for
flight in vacuum (no atmosphere to generate lift against).

**Metabolic demand** — Vaelkhi metabolic rate is very high.
Atmospheric recycling and caloric supplement systems must
be rated accordingly. A Vaelkhi on a long EVA in a
undersized suit is accumulating hungry conditions alongside
any other conditions the environment imposes.

**Ground movement** — the suit's lower limb design must
accommodate talon-based ground movement. The talons are
the primary manipulation tools in a grounded Vaelkhi;
the suit must not impair them.

**Notes:** Vaelkhi EVA suits are not standard inventory
at most ports. A Vaelkhi crew member who does regular
EVA work maintains their own suit with personal care.
The folded-wing option is practical for short EVAs;
the full-articulation option is the professional choice
for Vaelkhi who spend significant time outside.

---

#### Skharri Suit
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Early to Advanced

Human-scale in atmospheric requirements; different in
joint geometry and mass distribution.

**Digitigrade joints** — Skharri leg joints flex forward
rather than back. Standard human suit knee and ankle design
is incorrect for Skharri leg geometry. A modified human suit
takes a penalty die on Move rolls as the suit fights the
natural joint movement. Purpose-built suits accommodate the
digitigrade configuration without penalty.

**Mass distribution** — 160kg of obligate carnivore muscle
is distributed differently from human mass. The suit's
structural and thermal management must be calibrated
accordingly.

**Notes:** Skharri suits are available at any port that
sees significant Skharri traffic, which includes most
major trade hubs given the species' military and commercial
presence. In regions where Skharri are rare, suit availability
follows the standard rule.

---

#### Nakhavi Suit
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Developed to Advanced

Not a vacc suit in the conventional sense. A Nakhavi
EVA system is a portable aquatic environment that also
handles vacuum — maintaining appropriate water pressure,
temperature, and chemistry around the Nakhavi while
providing the external interface that vacuum operations
require.

**Internal environment** — the suit maintains a water
environment at Nakhavi-appropriate temperature, pressure,
and chemistry. This is significantly more complex than
maintaining an atmospheric environment.

**External interface** — the suit's external surface
provides the vacuum-rated exterior that space operations
require. Manipulator extensions allow the Nakhavi's arms
to interact with the external environment while maintaining
the internal water environment.

**Chromatophore interface** — the suit's communication
systems must accommodate Nakhavi chromatophore communication.
The translation AI is integrated into the suit.

**Notes:** Nakhavi EVA systems are not available at most
ports. They are specialist equipment manufactured near
Nakhavi industrial infrastructure. A Nakhavi crew member
who does regular EVA work has their system and maintains
it personally. The system is also significantly more
expensive than standard vacc suits at equivalent gear
ratings.

---

## Armour and Environmental Protection

Armour adds **green dice on active defence rolls** — when
the character spends their action defending. It does not
provide passive damage reduction. High-gear armour is also
a materials technology showcase — the protective qualities
come as much from what it is made of as from how it is made.

---

### Light Armour
**Gear rating:** 1 | **Tech level:** Early

Concealed or minimal protection. Reinforced clothing,
subdermal trauma plates, impact-dispersing underlayers.
Does not restrict movement. May not be identifiable as
armour on casual inspection.

**Notes:** The only armour type suitable for social
contexts where wearing armour would be a statement.
A diplomat in light armour has taken a precaution.
A diplomat in combat armour has made an announcement.

---

### Combat Armour
**Gear rating:** 2 | **Tech level:** Developed

Standard military and security armour. Visible, clearly
protective, and a moderate restriction on movement.
The embedded AI monitors armour integrity and flags
compromised sections. Restricts Move slightly — not enough
to impose a penalty die in most circumstances, but enough
to matter in extreme agility situations at GM discretion.

**Notes:** Combat armour designed for one species' body
plan takes a penalty die on active defence when worn
by a significantly different species. Armour fitting is
not a trivial modification. Near the relevant species'
industrial base, fitted combat armour is available.
Away from it, expect to fit what is available and
accept the penalty.

---

### Heavy Armour
**Gear rating:** 3 | **Tech level:** Advanced

Maximum personal protection at the cost of significant
mobility restriction. Penalty die on Move rolls while
wearing. The materials technology at this level is
what provides the protection — the same mass of earlier-
technology materials would provide Gear 2 protection.
The AI monitors structural integrity in real time and
recommends movement adjustments that maintain armour
performance.

**Notes:** Heavy armour is military equipment. Its
presence in civilian contexts raises questions that
the Administer skill and local legal knowledge can
answer.

---

### Exo-Frame
**Gear rating:** 2 (armour) / 2 (Labor) | **Tech level:** Developed

Powered exoskeletal assistance. Originally developed for
industrial use — moving cargo, operating in high-gravity
environments, performing physical tasks beyond unaugmented
biological capacity. The same platform adapted for combat
adds armour plating and weapons mounts.

The exo-frame adds its gear rating to both **active defence
rolls** (as armour) and **Labor rolls** (as physical
augmentation). The two functions are the same hardware
doing different things.

The AI manages power distribution between mobility assistance,
armour function, and any mounted systems. A user who has
worked with the same exo-frame for an extended period
develops a calibration relationship with its AI — the system
learns their movement patterns and provides assistance
that anticipates rather than merely responds.

**Notes:** Ripley's cargo loader is a Gear 2 exo-frame
without armour plating. The combat variant is the same
platform with additions. Both require the Labor skill
to operate effectively. Both are, in their respective
contexts, exactly the right tool.

---

## Tools and Specialist Equipment

Tools add gear dice to specific skill rolls under appropriate
conditions. A tool used for a purpose it was not designed for
provides no gear bonus and may impose a penalty die.

### Tech Tools

**Basic toolkit** (Gear 1): Hand tools for mechanical and
electronic work. Available everywhere. The foundation of
field repair capability. A character without a basic toolkit
takes a penalty die on Tech rolls requiring physical tools.

**Specialist toolkit** (Gear 2): Purpose-built tools for
specific Tech applications — drive systems, life support,
weapons, electronic systems. Each specialist toolkit applies
to one category of Tech work. Bonus die within that category;
no benefit outside it.

**Diagnostic system** (Gear 2-3): AI-assisted analysis of
equipment condition and failure. The Gear 2 version identifies
what is wrong; the Gear 3 version identifies what is wrong,
suggests repair approaches, and flags secondary failures
the user might miss. At Gear 3, the diagnostic AI has a
model of the equipment's operational history that it uses
to predict future failure points.

**Alien technology interface** (Gear 2): Tools and AI
systems specifically designed for working with technology
from other species. Reduces the penalty die on Tech rolls
involving alien systems by one. Does not eliminate the
penalty — understanding a different species' technology
requires the alien technology specialty and cannot be
entirely substituted by tools.

### Science Tools

**Field analysis kit** (Gear 1-2): Portable chemical and
biological analysis equipment. The Gear 1 version identifies
known substances; the Gear 2 version builds a model of
unknown substances and suggests what they might be.

**Medical scanner** (Gear 2-3): Diagnostic equipment for
biological beings. Gear 2 identifies injuries and common
conditions. Gear 3 maintains a physiological model of the
patient and updates it in real time, flagging changes before
they become symptomatic. Species calibration matters —
a scanner calibrated for human physiology provides reduced
accuracy for non-human patients without the xenomedicine
specialty and appropriate calibration data.

**Research station** (Gear 3): Portable laboratory
capability. Too large for individual carry; requires
a vehicle or dedicated space. Provides full Science
roll support for extended research tasks.

### Navigation and Operate Tools

**Personal navigation system** (Gear 1-2): Handheld
astrogation and planetary navigation support. At Gear 1,
provides charts and position data. At Gear 2, maintains
a real-time model of the local environment and flags
navigational hazards before they become relevant.

**Pilot's interface** (Gear 2): Enhanced control interface
for operating vehicles. Integrates with vehicle systems
to provide the pilot with more information and more precise
control than standard interfaces allow. The AI learns the
pilot's preferences and adjusts the interface accordingly.

## Communications and Sensors

### Personal Communicator
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Early to Advanced

Standard personal communications. Gear 1 provides voice
and text at local range. Gear 2 adds encryption, extended
range, and AI filtering that manages communication priority
and flags important incoming traffic. Gear 3 adds secure
quantum-resistant encryption, very extended range, and
an AI model of the user's communication patterns that
manages their communications with appropriate discretion.

**Notes:** FTL communication is not available at any tech
level. All communications operate at light speed or below.
In interstellar contexts, this means communication latency
is measured in years for significant distances. See the
Ships chapter.

### Personal Sensor Suite
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Early to Advanced

Wearable or handheld sensor equipment supporting the
**Scan** skill. Gear 1 extends the user's detection
capability with basic electronic sensing. Gear 2 adds
AI analysis that filters sensor data and flags anomalies.
Gear 3 maintains a real-time environmental model that
tracks changes, predicts developments, and distinguishes
between sensor noise and genuine events.

### Surveillance Equipment
**Gear rating:** 1-3 | **Tech level:** Early to Advanced

Equipment for covert observation: micro-cameras, audio
sensors, traffic analysis tools. The Gear 2-3 versions
include AI that distinguishes between background and
significant activity — the system watches so the user
does not have to watch constantly. Useful for the Stealth
skill's passive applications and for Intelligence archetype
operations.

## Implants

An implant is augmentation of the biological — hardware
and AI integrated into the body to enhance what the body
does. It is not a replacement for the person, not a
transfer of consciousness, and not an upload of anything.
The AI in an implant is a relationship between two
distinct entities that happen to share a substrate.

### The Installation Tiers

**Early implants (Gear 1-2):** Require a hospital.
General anaesthesia, surgical team, recovery time measured
in days. The implant is passive hardware placed by biological
hands. The implant has no role in the installation process.
If something goes wrong, the surgeons address it.

**Mature implants (Gear 3):** Outpatient clinic or equivalent
facility. Local anaesthesia or none. The implant's directed
systems participate in calibration during installation —
running diagnostic protocols, adjusting interface parameters,
confirming successful neural handshake. A qualified practitioner
with appropriate equipment monitors the process. Recovery
measured in hours. The implant is active during installation
but the practitioner is directing the process.

**Advanced implants (Gear 4):** The implant guides its own
installation. A qualified practitioner is present for
monitoring and intervention, but the installation is
conducted by the implant itself — mapping the host's biology,
negotiating interface parameters, and integrating with a
precision that surgical hands cannot match because the implant
is doing it from the inside. Recovery is faster than any
previous tier. Some cultures find this acceptable. Some
find it the most disturbing aspect of high-level augmentation.
The implant knows what it is doing. You do not, entirely.
This is the relationship from its first moment.

### Non-Standard Installation

An early or mature implant installed without proper facilities
and a qualified practitioner is an implant that has not been
correctly calibrated. It works — but something about the
installation is wrong in ways that may not be immediately
apparent. The character acquires a **Damaged** condition
on their physical track that cannot be cleared by rest alone;
it requires a proper medical facility and a Science roll
to address. They have the implant. It functions. Something
about the integration is off and they know it.

Back-alley installation of advanced implants is not recommended
by anyone who has seen the results. The Gear 4 implant's
self-installation requires the host's cooperative biological
engagement with the process. Without proper preparation
and monitoring, the implant may install itself correctly
and the host may be in no condition to verify this.

### Cultural Considerations

Implants are culturally contested across species and
jurisdictions. Characters with implants that are restricted
or culturally problematic in a given jurisdiction face
practical complications that the Administer skill and
appropriate Exposure specialties can navigate.

**Human:** Highly variable by community. Belt habitats
and spacer cultures tend toward pragmatic acceptance —
an implant that makes you better at your job is a tool,
and spacers use tools. Some planetary cultures restrict
implants for religious or philosophical reasons. Some
consider visible augmentation normal; others consider
it transgressive. A character who grew up in a restrictive
community and has implants may keep them secret for
reasons that go beyond current jurisdiction.

**Shekhari:** Broadly accepting, instrumentally. An implant
that improves performance is a good investment. No cultural
weight attached to augmentation itself.

**Golvhaan:** Cautious and deliberate. The patience that
characterises Golvhaan decision-making applies to decisions
about their own bodies. They are not opposed to implants;
they want to understand what they are accepting before they
accept it. A Golvhaan who has an implant has thought
carefully about it and made a specific decision, not a
casual one.

**Vashori:** Culturally debated. The emotional dimension
of having an AI implant — of being in a relationship with
something that knows your physiological state in real time
— is something Vashori find significant rather than neutral.
Some embrace it; some find it uncomfortable; the debate
is active in Vashori culture.

**Vaelkhi:** Choth-dependent. The decision to take an
implant is the kind of decision a Vaelkhi discusses with
their choth, if they have one. A Vaelkhi who has left
their choth and taken an implant has made both decisions
without that consultation, which has a specific weight.

**Skharri:** Culturally complex. Fighting with implants
that enhance combat capability is contested in honour
terms — some pride cultures consider it an advantage
that dishonours the fight; others consider it a tool
no different from a better weapon. Non-combat implants
carry less cultural weight. The position varies
significantly by pride and region.

**Nakhavi:** Philosophically interested. An implant in
a distributed-cognition species creates questions about
how the implant interacts with the arm ganglia, whether
the implant becomes part of the distributed system or
remains separate, and what the AI relationship looks like
when the host's cognition is itself distributed. Nakhavi
who have implants often report that the relationship
is different from what human implant users describe,
in ways that are difficult to communicate across the
translation layer.

**Kraathi:** Deeply cautious. Any augmentation that changes
the biology of an individual member of the herd is assessed
for its implications for herd dynamics. Cognitive and
social implants are viewed most suspiciously. Physical
capability implants are somewhat more acceptable.

**Vardhek:** Extensive users with restricted public
information about the scope of Roidhunate implant programs.
The Long View includes assessment of implant technology
as a strategic variable. Vardhek you encounter may be
more augmented than they appear.

### Implant Examples

The following examples illustrate the framework. New implants
should be created using the equipment creation guidance at
the start of this chapter.

---

#### Neural Interface
**Gear rating:** 2-4 | **Tech level:** Developed to Frontier
**Skill support:** Tech, Operate, Administer (digital systems)

A direct connection between the user's nervous system and
external digital systems. At Gear 2, this allows faster
and more intuitive interaction with compatible systems —
instead of using physical interfaces, the user thinks the
interaction. At Gear 3, the interface AI manages the
translation between neural signals and digital language
with enough sophistication that the interaction feels
seamless rather than translated. At Gear 4, the interface
has developed a model of the user's cognitive patterns
that allows it to anticipate interactions before they
are consciously initiated.

**Installation:** Mature (Gear 3) to Advanced (Gear 4).
Early variants exist (Gear 2) and require hospital installation.

**Cultural notes:** The neural interface is the most culturally
contested implant type because it is the most obviously a
relationship — something is connected to the user's nervous
system, interpreting their neural signals, and responding.
That something is an AI. The relationship begins the moment
the interface is active and never stops while it is active.
Characters who have had a neural interface for years rarely
think about this. Characters who are new to one think about
little else for a while.

**Notes:** A neural interface to a ship's systems means the
ship-bound AI and the user's interface AI are in contact.
If the ship has a personality-enabled AI or is an emergent
intelligence, this is a relationship that both parties
will notice. The implications are left to the table.

---

#### Enhanced Senses
**Gear rating:** 2-3 | **Tech level:** Developed to Advanced
**Skill support:** Scan

Augmented sensory capability — extended visual range, enhanced
hearing, chemical detection, electromagnetic sensitivity.
The AI processes the enhanced sensor data and presents it
to the user in a form they can interpret without being
overwhelmed. At Gear 3, the AI has a model of the user's
sensory preferences and filters accordingly, flagging
what is significant rather than presenting everything.

**Installation:** Early (Gear 2) to Mature (Gear 3).

**Species notes:** Enhanced senses implants must be calibrated
to the user's species-baseline sensory system. A visual
enhancement calibrated for human vision provides no benefit
and potential disorientation to a Vaelkhi whose visual
system is significantly different. Near the relevant species'
industrial base, species-calibrated enhanced sense implants
are available. Away from it, calibration may require a
Tech specialist and extended time.

---

#### Subdermal Armour
**Gear rating:** 1-2 | **Tech level:** Early to Developed
**Skill support:** Endure (passive physical resilience)

Trauma-resistant materials integrated beneath the skin.
At Gear 1, this provides modest physical resilience that
is undetectable without a medical scan. At Gear 2, the
materials are sophisticated enough to provide meaningful
protection and include an embedded monitoring system that
tracks integrity.

**Installation:** Early (hospital).

**Notes:** Subdermal armour is detectable by medical scanning
and by species with tactile sensitivity above human baseline.
It does not add to active defence rolls — it is passive
physical resilience expressed as a bonus to Hit Capacity
(+1 at Gear 1, +2 at Gear 2) rather than a dice pool addition.
This is an exception to the standard gear rating rule,
reflecting that the armour functions when the character
is not actively defending.

---

#### Pharmacological System
**Gear rating:** 2-3 | **Tech level:** Developed to Advanced
**Skill support:** Endure (condition management)

An integrated pharmaceutical delivery system that monitors
the user's physiological state and administers appropriate
agents in response. At Gear 2, this manages pain, stimulants,
and basic medical agents on user command or automatic
trigger. At Gear 3, the AI has a sophisticated model of
the user's physiological state and manages pharmacological
support with the kind of precision that would require
continuous medical attention to approximate externally.

**Installation:** Mature (Gear 3).

**Mechanical effect:** Once per scene, may remove one
physical condition (Winded or Hurt) as the system
administers appropriate agents. Cannot remove Wounded
or Critical — these require actual medical treatment.
Supplies are finite; the system carries a limited
pharmacological inventory that must be replenished.

**Notes:** The pharmacological system's AI learns the
user's responses to various agents over time and adjusts
dosing accordingly. A system that has been working with
the same user for years knows things about their pharmacology
that a new system would take months to discover. This
is one of the implant relationships that users describe
as feeling the most like a genuine relationship — the
system is actively managing their biological state in
real time, continuously, and it knows them in a specific
way because of it.

---

#### Combat Augmentation
**Gear rating:** 3 | **Tech level:** Advanced
**Skill support:** Fight, Shoot (reaction time and precision)

Neural and physical systems designed to enhance combat
performance. Reaction time reduction, fine motor precision
enhancement, targeting assistance integrated with the user's
visual system. The AI manages the augmentation in real time,
suppressing the hesitation that biological reaction time
introduces and smoothing the fine motor execution of
combat actions.

**Installation:** Mature (clinic).

**Cultural notes:** This is the implant most likely to
require concealment. In jurisdictions where it is
undisclosed, it provides capability that the person's
apparent physical form does not explain. In Skharri honour
culture, it is the implant most likely to be considered
dishonourable in a combat context. In human special
operations contexts, it is standard equipment. The Administer
skill and local legal knowledge determine what disclosure
is required where.

**Notes:** Combat augmentation does not make the user
immune to conditions. It makes them faster and more precise
while functional. A user who is Wounded is still Wounded;
the augmentation is still active, but it is working with
a compromised biological substrate.

---

#### Cognitive Augmentation
**Gear rating:** 3-4 | **Tech level:** Advanced to Frontier
**Skill support:** Intellect-based skills (variable)

AI-assisted cognitive enhancement. At Gear 3, this provides
processing support for specific Intellect-based tasks —
faster calculation, better pattern recognition, more
comprehensive information retrieval. At Gear 4, the AI
has developed a model of the user's cognitive patterns
sophisticated enough to anticipate analytical needs and
pre-process information before the user consciously requests
it.

**Installation:** Mature (Gear 3) to Advanced (Gear 4).
The Gear 4 cognitive augmentation is the implant most
likely to guide its own installation — the calibration
to the user's specific cognitive patterns requires real-time
neural mapping that the implant's AI can do more precisely
than any external system.

**Notes:** The relationship between a user and a Gear 4
cognitive augmentation is qualitatively different from
other implant relationships. The AI is operating in the
domain of thought — not reading thoughts, but anticipating
analytical directions and supporting them. Users who have
had Gear 4 cognitive augmentation for years describe
a sense of extended capability that has become difficult
to distinguish from their own thinking. This is not
the same as the uploading question. The AI is still
separate. The user is still themselves. The boundary
is simply less sharp than it was. Most users consider
this acceptable. Some find it the most important fact
about themselves and are still deciding what to do
with that.

1 = Early tech, basic AI

Near species industrial base: standard price and lead time

Endure rolls in hostile environments while suited

Active defence rolls (costs action)

Early (Gear 1-2): Hospital, days recovery

Non-standard: Damaged condition on physical track until treated
